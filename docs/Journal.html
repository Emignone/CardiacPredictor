INTERPRETATION

Number of injuries of people who went to angioplasty

The number of injuries in the different blood vessels is important since both this and the injured vessels determine the complexity of the surgery and therefore the choice of procedure

Extracorporeal circulation

Risk of immediate complications on patients who underwent surgery and doctors need to use extracorporeal circulation compared to those who did not have extracorporeal circulation. Extracorporeal circulation refers to the circulation of blood outside of the body through a machine that temporarily assumes an organ’s functions (eg. heart and lungs).

Hyperlactic patients with Post-Op Complications

Hyperlactatemia is the elevated quantity of lactate in blood. While a normal blood lactate level is between 0-2mmol/L, a patient with hyperlactatemia has a level above 3-5mmol/L. When this alteration arises in the operating room or very early in the ICU (early-onset hyperlactatemia), it is strongly associated with adverse outcomes and complications, while late-onset (6-12h in the ICU) is a benign alteration that spontaneously resolves itself within 24 hours. Between the 53 early-onset hyperlactic patients in surgery, 28 of them had complications.

Patients with severe LVEF values with Post-Op complications

Many studies have shown that LVEF values have relation with Post-Op surgery complications. Between the 11 patients in surgery with severe LVEF values, 7 of them had complications.

Previous angioplasty patients who had a re-angioplasty

We found that many patients who had undergone an angioplasty, had had another one. The cause for this phenomenon may be that the arteries are blocked or obstructed again (it could be the same or other arteries) which is not a rare risk of a first angioplasty.
